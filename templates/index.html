Pneumonia Detection

What is Pneumonia?

"Pneumonia is a lung infection caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, leading to inflammation of the air sacs (alveoli). These air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, making it difficult to breathe and causing symptoms like cough, fever, chest pain, and shortness of breath. Pneumonia can affect one or both lungs and varies in severity from mild to life-threatening, especially in young children, the elderly, and those with weakened immune systems."

Our Model

Description Model:

Pneumonia detection using deep learning on chest X-rays involves training convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to identify pneumonia from medical images. CNNs are effective at automatically learning patterns in X-rays, such as opacities in the lungs, which indicate infection. These models help in early diagnosis and reduce human error, making them valuable tools in clinical settings for pneumonia detection.
